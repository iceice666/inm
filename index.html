Spaces

Three ways the theme can live on a page.

Reading room

Long-form layouts stay soft and readable.

Bone keeps the page open, plum holds the copy with high contrast, and clay can punctuate quotes, links, and margin details without overpowering text.

“Warm palettes often drift muddy. This one stays clean because the contrast roles are disciplined.”

Product detail

Clay makes restrained calls to action.

Primary buttons feel tactile, not electric. That works well for premium commerce, bookings, and collection pages.

Quiet dashboard

Sage keeps utility surfaces calm.

Use sage for tags, filters, selection states, and background highlights where you want guidance without urgency.
